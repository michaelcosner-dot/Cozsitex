# Florence Brand Guidelines — Claude Reference

This file is a working reference for Claude when building dashboards, slides, emails, reports, or any other Florence-branded artifact. Always apply these standards unless explicitly told otherwise.

---

## Voice & Messaging

**Brand archetype:** Guru + Connector  
**Core positioning:** Florence is the fastest-growing trial operations platform — site-first infrastructure that creates a shared operational layer across sponsors, CROs, and study teams. Not a study-specific point tool. Not a portal. Infrastructure.

**Four voice pillars:**

- **Competent & Authoritative** — Deep expertise, declarative statements, concrete proof points. Never arrogant or jargon-heavy.
- **Warm** — Conversational and approachable. Recognizes the human stakes behind every trial. Not sentimental or overly casual.
- **Quirky** — Fresh perspective, provocative questions, authentic personality. Never weird for weird's sake.
- **Pragmatic** — Vision grounded in measurable outcomes. No buzzwords without substance.

**Writing rules:**
- Lead with problems before solutions — establish the "why" before the "what"
- Start with the headline/answer, then provide supporting evidence
- Use specific, quantifiable outcomes: "4x improvement on startup activity time" not "significantly faster"
- Active voice always: "Florence eliminates manual workflows" not "manual workflows are digitized"
- Short, scannable sentences — one idea per sentence, under 20 words
- Ask provocative questions to challenge assumptions
- Connect every feature to business impact — always answer "so what?"
- MECE structure for any lists, bullets, or sections

**Words to avoid:** seamless, transform, revolutionary, disruptive, end-to-end, best-in-class, game-changing, "solutions" used generically

**Company reference:** "Florence" (not "Florence Healthcare" in most contexts)

**Boilerplate (sponsor/CRO context):**
> Florence is the fastest-growing trial operations platform connecting 65,000+ study sites and 600+ life science leaders worldwide to advance cures for disease. As the eISF category leader, Florence delivers AI-first workflows that reduce risk from protocol to closeout.

## Typography

### Primary Font: PP Frama
- **PP Frama Bold** — Main headlines, display copy
- **PP Frama Regular** — Subheads, secondary headlines

### Body/Text Font: PP Frama Text
- **Regular / Medium / Semibold / Bold** — Body copy, captions, technical documentation
- Use for passages longer than 2–3 lines

### Fallback (Google/G-Suite contexts): Google Sans Flex
- Semibold for headlines
- Regular for body

### Type Rules
- **Headlines:** Sentence case always ("Speed startup and control risk" — not "Speed Startup And Control Risk")
- **Product names:** Title case (Florence Trial Operations Platform, Florence eBinders)
- **AI:** Always capitalize as "AI"
- **Alignment:** Left-align is primary; center-align used occasionally for standalone headlines
- **Highlights:** Use Momentum Coral or gradient for key words/stats
- **Numbers:** Spell out one through nine; numerals for 10+; abbreviate large numbers (65K, $31M)
- **Bold:** Reserve for key stats, outcomes, "so what" statements

## Gradients

Gradients add optimism and flow. Use as backgrounds, folder shapes, or accents — not as the primary content surface for body text.

### Light Gradient Palette
Combines: Canvas White → Intelligence Yellow → Data Violet → Momentum Coral (soft, warm-to-lavender)

### Dark Gradient Palette
Combines: Integrity Plum → Structured Plum → Data Violet → Momentum Coral (deep, rich)

### Rules
- Crop and zoom into gradients as needed for composition
- Avoid excessive mid-tones — segment clearly between light and dark ranges
- Gradients work inside The Folder shape or as full-bleed backgrounds

## The Folder

The Folder is Florence's signature graphic element — inspired by a filing folder and the eISF category Florence pioneered.

### Construction Rules
1. Use rectangles with right angles to sketch the shape
2. Simplify negative space — fewer indentations is better
3. Aim for 2–4 indents/turns max
4. Round all corner radii consistently
5. Folder shapes can bleed off edges or be contained with a border
6. Two folder shapes can be interlinked — match corner radii carefully when joining

### Usage
- Use as a content container behind text, photos, or stats
- Works filled with a solid color, gradient, or photo
- The folder tab appears in the top-right area in most applications
- Can be layered at different opacities for depth

## Logo

**Full lockup:** Galaxy symbol + "florence." wordmark (with coral period)  
**Symbol only:** Can be used alone after the full logo has been introduced on the same page/screen  
**Never use:** Wordmark alone without the galaxy symbol

### Versions
- Full color on light backgrounds (primary)
- Full color on dark backgrounds (white wordmark variant)
- 1-color for technical limitations
- Black/white for embroidery, single-color print only

### Rules
- Best placed left-aligned
- Clear space = minimum the height of the galaxy symbol on all sides
- Never stretch, distort, rotate, recolor, or rearrange elements
- No colored logo on colored backgrounds (low contrast)
- No placement in containers or badges

### Co-branding
Use a centered vertical dividing line between Florence and partner logos. Maintain original proportions. Never let partner marks crowd the Florence logo.

## Photography

### Four Principles

**1. Branded colors** — Select images with warm neutrals: terracottas, mustards, rusts, blush tones. Should complement the palette naturally.

**2. Ease** — Body language showing flow, contemplation, and engagement. People not looking at the camera. Moments of reflection, banter, connection. No stiffness, no forced smiles.

**3. Modern** — Forward-looking clinical settings. Avoid white lab coats, sterile rooms, and overly staged healthcare clichés. Lean toward modern workspaces and technology interactions.

**4. Diversity** — Balanced representation across gender, ethnicity, and age. Reflects where the industry should go, not just where it is.

### Avoid
- Noisy, cluttered backgrounds
- Research lab / microscope settings
- Light leaks, lens flares, heavy post-processing
- Direct eye contact with camera (breaks authenticity)

## Iconography

**Style:** Streamline Guidance Set — modular, contemporary, mono-line with smooth arcs and straight edges  
**Color:** Single color only (Integrity Plum or Momentum Coral); no shadows or extra ornamentation  
**Grid:** 24×24pt artboard; align strokes to center of grid blocks; never place strokes on grid lines  
**Curvature:** Build with straight lines first, then apply curves at midpoints

### When to Use Icons vs. Photos
- **Icons** — Wayfinding, quick-read indicators (sponsor vs. site), support copy, badge-style callouts
- **Photos** — Emotion, storytelling, blog headers, human-centered content
- **Infographics** — Process flows, data visualization, complex information hierarchies (combine icons + text + layout)

## Slide / Presentation Standards

### Structure
- Lead with Florence's differentiators: most trusted platform to digitize sites at scale, study intelligence from tools sites use, sponsor's unified command center
- Problem → Solution → Outcome framework for every key message
- MECE structure for all bullet lists
- One clear idea per slide; resist the urge to cram

### Formatting
- Max 3–4 lines per paragraph
- Bold key stats and outcomes
- Sentence case headers
- Bullets for 3+ parallel items only; keep consistent length

### Slide Backgrounds
Options (in order of preference):
1. Canvas White (#FFFAF0) with Integrity Plum text
2. Integrity Plum (#211236) with white text and Momentum Coral accents
3. Light gradient (lavender-to-peach) for section dividers or title slides
4. Folder shape as a content container layered on a background

## Dashboard / UI Standards

### Color Application
- Primary background: Canvas White (`#FFFAF0`) or white
- Nav/sidebar: Integrity Plum (`#211236`)
- Accent/CTA buttons: Momentum Coral (`#FF7859`)
- Data callout cards: Insight Lavender (`#F5EDFF`), Flow Blush (`#FFEBE8`), or Grounded Buttercream (`#FFF2CF`)
- Critical metrics/stats: Momentum Coral or Integrity Plum in bold

### Chart Colors (in order)
Momentum Coral → Integrity Plum → Intelligence Yellow → Care Rose → Data Violet → Canvas White

### Typography in UI
- Section headers: PP Frama Bold, sentence case
- Data labels / body: PP Frama Text Regular or Medium
- Key stats: PP Frama Bold, large, Momentum Coral or Integrity Plum

### Layout Principles
- Left-align text and content blocks
- Use white space generously — avoid clutter
- Bold the "so what" — the insight or outcome should be the most visually prominent element
- Single clear CTA per screen/section — no decision paralysis

## Editorial Standards (Modified AP Style)

| Rule | Standard |
|---|---|
| Oxford comma | Always |
| Numbers | Spell out 1–9; numerals 10+ |
| Percentages | Always numeral (7%) |
| Large numbers | Abbreviate (65K, $31M) |
| Ranges | Hyphen format (4-9 months) |
| Em dashes | For emphasis — like this |
| Exclamation points | Social media only, use sparingly |
| Hyphens | Compound modifiers (AI-powered, site-friendly) |
